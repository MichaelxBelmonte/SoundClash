# Contest Rules — Musicathon 2026

> Faithful transcription of the official **Musicathon 2026 Contest Rules** (23 sections · "Updated May 12" · Binding: Yes), as provided in the organizer's screenshots. The organizer's official published rules are the authoritative source; this file is a working copy for the team. See [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md) for how these rules translate into engineering constraints.

> ⚠️ **Date discrepancy (read this).** The rules are internally inconsistent on the deadline: the **Overview** says the hackathon runs **June 15 → 22** with submissions closing **23:59 CET, June 22**, but **Contest Period**, **Entry Requirements**, and **Winner Selection** all say **June 21, 2026 (23:59 CEST)**. Three of four sections say **June 21** — treat **21 June 2026, 23:59 CEST** as the working deadline and confirm with the organizer.

## 1. No Purchase Necessary
No purchase is necessary to enter or win. A purchase does not increase the chances of winning.

## 2. Overview
Musicathon 2026 is an 8-day online hackathon hosted by Musixmatch from **June 15 to June 22, 2026**. Submissions close at **23:59 CET on June 22, 2026**. Winners are announced on **June 30, 2026**. *(See date-discrepancy note above.)*

## 3. Eligibility (general)
Open to participants **18+ worldwide**. Employees of Musixmatch and their immediate families are not eligible for cash prizes but may participate non-competitively. **Teams may have up to four members.**

## 4. Eligibility (detailed)
Open to individuals who are at least 18 years of age (or the age of majority in their jurisdiction, whichever is higher) at the time of entry. Void where prohibited by applicable law.
The Contest is **not open** to residents of, or persons located in: **Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Russia, Belarus**, and the Ukrainian regions of **Crimea, Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia**. It is also **not open** to residents of **China, Brazil, or India** due to local prize-promotion regulations requiring filings the Organizer has not undertaken.
Employees of Musixmatch S.p.A. ("Organizer"), Replit, Inc. ("Sponsor"), their respective affiliates, subsidiaries, advertising and promotion agencies, suppliers, and their immediate family and/or household members are not eligible to participate or win.

## 5. Technical requirements
Every submission **must use at least one Musixmatch API surface**. Use of partner APIs (Songstats, LALAL.AI, Replit) is encouraged but not required. **Code must be your own** — open-source dependencies are fine, full project clones are not.

## 6. Agreement to Rules
By registering and participating, you agree to be fully bound by these Contest Rules and represent that you meet all eligibility requirements. You agree to accept the decisions of the Organizer as final and binding on all matters related to the Contest, including winner selection and prize awards.

## 7. Contest Period
The Musicathon runs from **June 15, 2026 at 00:00 CEST to June 21, 2026 at 23:59 CEST** ("Contest Period"). Entries must be submitted before the Contest Period ends. The Organizer reserves the right to modify or extend the Contest Period with reasonable notice to registered participants.

## 8. Submission requirements
Submissions must include: a **title**, a **one-liner**, a **description**, a **cover image**, a **working demo URL or 90-second demo video**, and a **public source repository**. Late submissions will not be reviewed.

## 9. Entry Requirements
To be eligible for prizes, all submitted projects must:
- Integrate and actively use the Musixmatch API in a **meaningful** way.
- Be submitted before the June 21, 2026 deadline.
- Include a working demo and a brief written description of the project.
- Be the **original work** of the participant(s).

Projects that do not fulfill all Contest requirements may be disqualified at the sole discretion of the Organizer. Participants may not use fraudulent methods or otherwise attempt to circumvent the rules. **Only one submission per participant or team** is permitted.

## 10. Judging criteria
Jury scores each submission on **Originality (25%)**, **Craft (25%)**, **Use of the Musixmatch API (25%)**, and **Impact (25%)**. Ties are broken by the head of jury.

## 11. Code of conduct
Be respectful in all event channels. Harassment of any kind is not tolerated and will result in disqualification.

## 12. Musixmatch API Usage
Registered participants will receive a Musixmatch API key with **Scale-plan access valid for the duration of the Contest Period (7 days)**. This key is for Contest use only and must not be shared, resold, or used for any purpose outside the Musicathon. The Organizer reserves the right to revoke API access for any participant found to be in violation of this rule or the Musixmatch API Terms of Service.

## 13. Intellectual property
Participants **retain all IP** in their submissions. By submitting, participants grant Musixmatch a non-exclusive worldwide license to publicly display their submission for the purpose of promoting Musicathon.

## 14. Content Usage Restrictions
All content accessed through the Musixmatch API — including but not limited to lyrics, metadata, audio analysis data, and any other proprietary content — is subject to the following restrictions:
- **No commercial use:** Content retrieved via the API may not be used for any commercial purpose. Projects submitted to the Musicathon are for demonstration and evaluation purposes only.
- **No downloading or storing:** Participants must not bulk-download, cache, scrape, or otherwise store API content beyond what is strictly necessary for **real-time display** within their project. **Persistent storage of Musixmatch content is strictly forbidden.**
- **No redistribution:** Content may not be redistributed, sublicensed, resold, or made available to third parties outside of the Contest submission process.
- **Compliance with copyright:** All music content accessed via the API is protected by copyright. Participants are responsible for ensuring their project respects applicable intellectual property laws.

Violation of these restrictions may result in **immediate disqualification and revocation of API access**.

## 15. Replit Credits
Registered participants may claim free Replit credits provided by the official sponsor, Replit, Inc. Use of Replit is entirely optional. Participants are free to build and deploy their projects using any tools, environments, or platforms of their choice. Replit credits are non-transferable and subject to Replit's standard terms of service.

## 16. Prizes & Odds
The Musicathon awards the following prizes:
- 🥇 **1st Place** — $5,000 cash + 1-year Musixmatch **Scale** plan subscription (free)
- 🥈 **2nd Place** — 1-year Musixmatch **Scale** plan subscription (free)
- 🥉 **3rd Place** — 1-year Musixmatch **Grow** plan subscription (free)

No cash substitution or prize transfer is permitted, except at the Organizer's sole discretion. All prize-related expenses, including any applicable federal, state, or local taxes, are the sole responsibility of the winner. Withholding tax (WHT) may be applicable to cash prizes depending on the winner's jurisdiction; winners are solely responsible for all tax obligations arising from the receipt of any prize. Acceptance of a prize constitutes permission for the Organizer to use the winner's name, likeness, and project for promotional purposes without further compensation, except where prohibited by law.

## 17. Winner Selection and Notification
Winners will be selected by the Musixmatch team after the submission deadline closes on June 21, 2026. Projects will be evaluated based on **creativity, technical execution, quality of Musixmatch API integration, and overall impact**. The Organizer shall have no liability for a winner's failure to receive notifications due to spam filters, incorrect contact information, or other communication failures. If a selected winner cannot be contacted, is found ineligible, or fails to claim the prize, the prize may be forfeited and an alternate winner selected at the Organizer's discretion.

## 18. Compliance
Receipt of any prize is conditioned upon compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations. Any violation of these Official Rules by a winner will result in disqualification and immediate forfeiture of all prizes and winner privileges, at the Organizer's sole discretion.

## What this means for Lyric Royale (engineering implications)
These map directly onto our build — full detail in [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md):
- **Must use Musixmatch meaningfully** (§5, §9) → lyrics + synced lyrics are the core game material. ✅
- **No persistent storage / no redistribution / real-time display only** (§14) → we persist **references only** (`track_id + line_index + round_type + seed`), regenerate lyric text **live**, and never store or share lyric text. See [`DATA_MODEL.md`](./DATA_MODEL.md).
- **Display copyright + non-commercial demo** (§14) → render `lyrics_copyright` and fire the tracking pixel/script on every lyric display.
- **API key is Contest-only, revocable** (§12) → server-side only, never shared/committed. See [`ARCHITECTURE.md`](./ARCHITECTURE.md) and [`AGENT_BRIEFING.md`](../AGENT_BRIEFING.md).
- **Submission package** (§8) → title, one-liner, description, cover image, demo URL or 90s video, public repo. Checklist in [`BUILD_PLAN.md`](./BUILD_PLAN.md).
- **Judging 25%×4** (§10) → Originality / Craft / Use of Musixmatch / Impact. Feature mapping in [`PRODUCT_SPEC.md`](./PRODUCT_SPEC.md).
- **Original work, one submission, teams ≤4** (§9, §3).
